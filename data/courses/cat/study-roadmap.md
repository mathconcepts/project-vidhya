# 90-Day CAT Study Roadmap

> **Goal**: Move from zero to a competitive CAT attempt in 3 months.
> **Time required**: 3–4 hours/day (weekdays) + 6–8 hours (weekends)
> **Best for**: Working professionals and final-year students targeting CAT in 3–4 months

---

## The Big Picture

| Phase | Weeks | Focus | Weekly Mocks |
|-------|-------|-------|-------------|
| Foundation | 1–4 | Core concepts, formulae, basic problem-solving | 1 sectional |
| Application | 5–8 | Mixed practice, speed building, weak area targeting | 1 full mock |
| Exam Mode | 9–12 | Full-length mocks, analysis, revision sprints | 2–3 full mocks |

**Key rule**: Don't spend more than 6 weeks on theory. CAT rewards practice, not reading.

## Phase 2: Application (Weeks 5–8)

### Week 5 — QA: Algebra + Geometry
- Days 1–3: Algebra (equations, inequalities, functions, progressions)
- Days 4–5: Geometry (triangles, circles, coordinate geometry)
- Weekend: 22-question QA mock + error analysis

### Week 6 — QA: Modern Math + Mixed
- Days 1–2: P&C + Probability
- Day 3: Mixed QA timed set (all topics)
- Days 4–5: 3 QA mocks, identify consistent weak area
- Weekend: **First full CAT mock** (120 min) + deep analysis

### Week 7 — VARC: RC Speed + VA Mastery
- Days 1–3: RC — 2 passages/day (increasing difficulty), strict timing
- Day 4: RC tone and inference questions (hardest type)
- Day 5: Full VA set — para-jumbles, summary, odd sentence under time pressure
- Weekend: VARC sectional + CAT 2022 paper VARC section

### Week 8 — DILR: Hard Sets + Speed
- Days 1–2: Novel LR set types (custom puzzles)
- Day 3: Set selection strategy — timed survey exercise
- Days 4–5: DILR from CAT 2021/2022 papers (actual difficulty level)
- Weekend: Full CAT mock + DILR-focused analysis

**End of Phase 2 checkpoint:**
- [ ] QA: 14+ correct in 40 min
- [ ] VARC: 16+ correct in 40 min
- [ ] DILR: 12+ correct in 40 min
- [ ] Overall: 42+ correct out of 66

## Phase 3: Exam Mode (Weeks 9–12)

### The Shift
Stop learning new concepts. Start optimising your approach.
- 2–3 full mocks per week
- Every mock followed by 90-minute analysis session
- Daily: 1 RC passage + 1 DILR set (maintenance)

### Week 9 — Mock Rhythm
- Mon–Thu: 1 hr daily — weak area drills (identified from Phase 2 analysis)
- Fri: Full mock (CAT 2023 paper if available)
- Sat: Full analysis — question-by-question review of wrong + skipped
- Sun: Rest or light revision

### Week 10 — Speed + Accuracy Calibration
- 3 full mocks this week
- Focus: attempt strategy — how many to attempt in each section?
- Experiment with 2–3 different attempt strategies
- Lock your personal strategy by end of week

### Week 11 — Pressure Simulation
- Mocks at exam timing (morning slot: 9am, afternoon: 2pm)
- Add exam-day pressure: no breaks, no phone, exam conditions
- Focus on: not panicking on hard sets, time management per section

### Week 12 — Final Sprint
- Days 1–3: High-yield revision — formula sheet, PYQ patterns
- Day 4: Light mock (2 sections, not full)
- Day 5: Nothing new — confidence consolidation
- Day 6 (day before): 3-hour light revision, early sleep
- **Exam day**: Trust the process.

## The One Formula

**Score = (Accuracy × Attempts) − Penalty**

Optimise for accuracy first. Then attempts. Never sacrifice accuracy for attempt count.

A 70% accuracy on 50 attempts = 35 correct − 5 wrong = 30 net marks.
A 85% accuracy on 42 attempts = 35.7 correct − 2.1 wrong = 33.6 net marks.

**The second student wins with fewer attempts.**
