## Why GATE EM Matters More Than You Think

Let's do the math.

GATE 2024 had 65 questions for 100 marks. Engineering Mathematics contributed roughly 13 marks. If you score 100% in EM while your competitor scores 0%, you gain a full letter grade in the cutoff ranking.

EM is also the most predictable section. The syllabus hasn't changed in 10 years. The question patterns repeat. The formulas are fixed. Unlike core subjects where new research can surprise you, EM is a known, winnable game.

The problem? Most candidates either (a) skip EM assuming it's too hard, or (b) study everything superficially and score 40–50% when they could score 80–90%.

This guide fixes that.

## The 90-Day Plan

### Month 1: Build the Foundation (Days 1–30)

**Weeks 1–2: High-Priority Topics**

*Days 1–7: Linear Algebra*
- Matrices, determinants, rank
- Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
- System of equations (Gauss elimination)
- Practice: 20 MCQs from 2015–2024

*Days 8–14: Calculus*
- Limits, continuity, differentiability
- Partial derivatives, maxima/minima
- Multiple integrals
- Practice: 20 MCQs from past papers

*Days 15–21: Probability & Statistics*
- Probability axioms, Bayes' theorem
- Standard distributions (Binomial, Poisson, Normal)
- Mean, variance, standard deviation
- Practice: 20 MCQs

*Days 22–30: Discrete Mathematics*
- Propositional logic, set theory
- Combinatorics, permutations
- Relations and functions
- Practice: 20 MCQs

**End of Month 1 checkpoint:** You should be able to solve 70% of PYQs from these 4 topics.

### Month 2: Medium-Priority Topics (Days 31–60)

*Days 31–40: Differential Equations*

*Days 57–60: Graph Theory*
- Trees, spanning trees
- Eulerian and Hamiltonian graphs

**End of Month 2 checkpoint:** Attempt a full mock GATE EM section. Target: 70–75% score.

## The 5 Mistakes That Kill EM Scores

**1. Skipping the formula sheet**

Every GATE EM question is ultimately about applying the right formula correctly. Build a one-page formula sheet per topic. Review it every morning for the last 4 weeks.

**2. Solving questions without understanding**

If you can't explain *why* the Newton-Raphson iteration works, you'll miss the GATE variant that changes one element. Always ask "what is this really doing?"

**3. Avoiding tough topics entirely**

Differential Equations and Complex Variables look scary but have predictable patterns. 2–3 marks from these topics are achievable with targeted practice.

**4. Not timing yourself**

GATE EM questions must be solved in 2–3 minutes on average. Practice under time pressure from Month 2 onward.

**5. No negative marking awareness**

With -0.67 for wrong answers, a random guess costs you. If you're more than 60% confident, attempt it. Otherwise, skip and come back.
