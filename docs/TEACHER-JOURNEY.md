# Teacher Journey — Vidhya

*Teachers are end-users too. This doc maps how a human teacher
experiences Vidhya from first login through daily teaching, and how
the student-teacher relationship is modeled when a real human — not
just the AI — is in the loop.*

**Companion doc:** `docs/USER-JOURNEY.md` covers the student and
admin journeys. This document focuses on teachers as a distinct
end-user persona and redefines how students experience teaching.

---

## 1. The core mental model

Vidhya has always had an "AI teacher" — every lesson, every chat
response, every worked example is generated by the pedagogical engine.
What the v2.9.3 refinement adds is the recognition that **students
with a human teacher assigned experience a different app than students
studying alone.**

```
Student  →  Who is my teacher?
              │
              ├─  No human teacher assigned
              │     └─  AI is the teacher (default, v2.5+ behavior)
              │
              └─  Human teacher assigned (admin sets taught_by)
                    └─  Human teacher + AI together
                         - Human sets what to study
                         - AI delivers the teaching
                         - Student's progress is visible to human
                         - Human can send cohort announcements
```

Critically: a student with a human teacher doesn't lose AI access.
They gain *both*. The human is the curator; the AI is the deliverer.

### Who can be the "teacher" for a student

1. **The AI itself** — default for every student. Always available.
   Never absent. Vidhya's cognitive core handles the teaching.

2. **A human with the teacher role** — assigned by an admin via
   `/admin/users`. Becomes the student's human mentor. Can see the
   student's cognitive health, send the student announcements, and
   push concepts to the student's review queue.

Both exist simultaneously for human-taught students. The student
can ask Vidhya anything, at any time, with or without their human
teacher involved. The human teacher is *additive*, not substitutive.

## 2. The teacher's journey

Ramya teaches engineering math at a coaching institute. Her admin
deploys Vidhya and assigns 20 students to her. This is her journey.

### Stage T1: First login as a teacher

Ramya signs in with Google. She was previously promoted by the admin,
so she lands as `role: teacher`.

**Pain points before v2.9.3:**

| # | Pain | Severity |
|---|------|:-:|
| T1.1 | **She lands on `/chat` (student homepage)** — no indication she's a teacher, no teacher-specific features shown | HIGH |
| T1.2 | **She has to know to visit `/teacher/roster`** to see her students | HIGH |
| T1.3 | **The roster shows observation data but no action paths.** She sees "student struggling with eigenvalues" but no way to DO anything about it. | HIGH |

**Fixed in v2.9.3:**

- Teachers signing in are greeted with a "Teaching mode" indicator in the nav. A new `/teaching` route shows their teaching dashboard by default.
- The teaching dashboard surfaces what to teach next, based on cohort data.
- Every observation comes with an action: "push this concept to their review queue," "send a class announcement about this," "flag this as next-class priority."

### Stage T2: Preparing for the next class

Ramya's next class is in 2 days. She wants to know: **what should I
teach?**

**What she gets in v2.9.3:**

The teaching dashboard at `/teaching` surfaces:

1. **Next-class recommendation** — "Your cohort's weakest concept
   right now is `eigenvalues` (avg mastery 38%, 12 of 20 students
   below threshold). Here's a teaching brief."

2. **Teaching brief per concept** (on tap) — actionable, not just
   data:
   - The 3 common misconceptions your cohort has shown (from error
     taxonomy signals)
   - 3 worked examples ranked by difficulty for your cohort's level
   - 2 problems to discuss in class
   - 2 mini-exercises to use for live formative assessment
   - Suggested talking points: "Start with the geometric intuition
     before the algebraic — 8 of 20 students show representation-mode
     'algebraic' preference and benefit from a visual anchor first."

3. **Cohort trends** — which concepts are trending up (mastery gains
   this week), which are plateauing, which are regressing.

4. **Attention list** — students flagged for 1:1 intervention
   (5+ consecutive failures or emotional state: frustrated/anxious).

Everything on this dashboard is generated from existing data. No
new content, no LLM call at render time. The brief is a *different view*
of the bundle + cohort mastery, which the teacher can act on.

### Stage T3: During class

Ramya teaches eigenvalues using the teaching brief she prepared. After
class, she wants to **reinforce what she taught** by pushing practice
to her students.

**What she gets in v2.9.3:**

From the concept's teaching brief page, she can:

- **Push to review queue** — a single click adds the concept to all 20
  students' "due for review" SM-2 queues. Students see it on their
  home. No email spam, no notification — it shows up when they next
  open Vidhya.

- **Send a class announcement** — short text (e.g., "We'll continue
  eigenvalues tomorrow. Make sure you've done problem 3 on page 47 of
  the textbook."). Appears as a dismissible banner on each student's
  home. One announcement at a time per teacher (new one replaces old).

Both actions are permission-based. Students see *what* their teacher
pushed and *why*. Nothing is done covertly.

### Stage T4: Between classes

Ramya checks her dashboard a few times a week. She's looking for:

- **Who's slipping behind?** — the attention list shows students
  whose mastery is regressing or who are in frustrated state
- **What's my cohort ready for?** — concepts trending up indicate
  when she can move to harder material
- **Who needs a 1:1?** — flagged students with attention reasons

**What she gets in v2.9.3:**

Previously (v2.9.0), the teacher roster showed aggregate counts. In
v2.9.3, each flagged student's row shows an **action button**: "Send
a check-in" — generates a pre-filled template ("Hi {name}, I noticed
you've been working hard on {concept}. Want to schedule a 15-min
call?") that the teacher can copy/paste into their preferred comms
channel (WhatsApp, SMS, email).

We deliberately do NOT build in-app DMs for this. Teachers and
students already have communication channels. We give teachers a
prompt and a pre-filled message; we let them use whatever medium they
already use with their students.

## 3. The student's perspective when they have a human teacher

Student Maya (from `docs/USER-JOURNEY.md`) has been assigned to
teacher Ramya by the admin. How does Maya's experience change?

### Addition 1: "Your teacher" card on home

When Maya opens Vidhya, her home now includes a compact card:

```
Your teacher: Ramya K.
  ▸ ramya@institute.com
  ▸ can see your overall progress (not individual answers)
  See what Ramya sees →  (opens a preview of the teacher's-view of her)
```

This makes the relationship explicit. Maya knows:
- Who her teacher is
- What her teacher can see (aggregate mastery, flagged concerns)
- What her teacher CANNOT see (raw answers, emotional state details,
  question transcripts)

Privacy transparency is non-negotiable. Maya being able to preview
what Ramya sees is how we build trust.

### Addition 2: Announcement banner

When Ramya sends a class announcement, it appears on Maya's home as
a subtle, dismissible banner at the top. Example:

```
📢 From Ramya: "We'll continue eigenvalues tomorrow. Make sure
you've done problem 3 on page 47 of the textbook."
(Dismiss)
```

Dismissed announcements are gone forever. Next announcement replaces
this one.

### Addition 3: Push-to-review items marked visibly

If Ramya pushes `eigenvalues` to the review queue, Maya sees in her
review-due card:

```
Due for review:
  ▸ eigenvalues  (Ramya pushed this to you)
  ▸ integration-by-parts  (SM-2 scheduled)
```

The "(Ramya pushed this to you)" tag distinguishes teacher-pushed from
SM-2-automatic reviews. Maya knows *why* this concept is on her list.

### What DOESN'T change for self-study students

Students with no human teacher assigned (`taught_by: null`) see
exactly what they saw before. No "Your teacher" card, no
announcements, no push-to-review tags. They're still studying with
the AI. The experience is unchanged.

## 4. What v2.9.3 ships

### New pages & components

- **`/teaching`** — teachers' dashboard (what to teach, how to teach,
  cohort trends)
- **`YourTeacherCard`** — student home component, shown only when
  `taught_by` is set
- **`AnnouncementBanner`** — dismissible teacher-to-cohort
  announcement on student home
- **Teaching brief drawer** — per-concept actionable view with push-to-
  review + announcement actions

### New backend endpoints

```
GET  /api/teaching/next-class
    Teacher's next-class recommendation based on cohort mastery

GET  /api/teaching/brief/:concept_id
    Full teaching brief for a concept — misconceptions, examples,
    problems, exercises, talking points — all filtered to the
    requesting teacher's cohort

POST /api/teaching/push-to-review
    Add a concept to all of the teacher's students' SM-2 queues
    Body: { concept_id }

POST /api/teaching/announcement
    Post a class announcement (overrides previous one)
    Body: { text }  max 280 chars

GET  /api/teaching/announcement (as a student)
    Returns the announcement from your taught_by teacher, or null

GET  /api/student/my-teacher
    Returns the student's assigned human teacher (id, name, picture,
    email) or null
```

### Storage

- **`.data/teaching-announcements.json`** — `{ teacher_id: { text,
  posted_at }, ... }` — one active announcement per teacher, latest
  replaces. Uses shared `flat-file-store`.
- **User record extended** — `pushed_reviews` array per user, each
  entry: `{ concept_id, pushed_by_teacher_id, pushed_at }`. Surfaces
  on the review-due card. Dismissed or completed entries are pruned.

### Documentation

- `docs/TEACHER-JOURNEY.md` — this document
- `docs/USER-JOURNEY.md` — updated to reference the teacher persona
  and the two teaching modes

## 5. Design principles (teacher-specific)

Extending the 6 principles from `docs/USER-JOURNEY.md` Part 5, with
3 teacher-specific principles:

7. **Teachers get data with actions, never data alone.** Every
   observation — "student is struggling" — must come with an action
   path — "send a check-in," "push this to review," "prepare a brief."
8. **Don't replace teacher-student communication, augment it.**
   Teachers already have WhatsApp/SMS/email with students. We don't
   build in-app DMs. We give teachers pre-filled templates they copy.
9. **Transparency between human teacher and student is non-negotiable.**
   Students can see what their teacher sees. Teachers can see what
   students see. No surveillance, no hidden data flows.

## 6. Explicit non-goals

These are not "deferred" — they're deliberate out-of-scope decisions
that define what Vidhya is and isn't as a product.

- **No in-app messaging/DMs.** Teachers use their existing comms
  channels (WhatsApp, SMS, email). We give pre-filled templates.
- **No gradebook or assignment tracking.** Vidhya is a learning
  platform, not a classroom management system.
- **No teacher-authored content.** Teachers use the existing bundle
  + curriculum. The curriculum YAML format (see
  `docs/CURRICULUM-FRAMEWORK.md`) is the extension point.
- **No multi-teacher-per-student.** A student has at most one human
  teacher. Simplification, not a limitation to revisit.
- **No LLM-generated teaching briefs.** Briefs are composed from the
  existing content bundle + cohort mastery data. No additional LLM
  cost at render time. This is a feature, not a limitation.

## 7. The "state of bliss" checkpoints for teachers

Adding to the 5 student/admin checkpoints in `docs/USER-JOURNEY.md`,
the three moments that make a teacher feel the app is on their side:

### Teacher bliss 1 — First teaching brief

Teacher clicks into a concept brief and thinks: "*oh — this is prep
work someone else did for me*." The common misconceptions their
cohort actually has, the worked examples at the right level, the
talking points based on representation-mode preference.

### Teacher bliss 2 — First attention-flag catch

A student slips into `frustrated` state. The dashboard surfaces it.
The teacher sends a pre-filled check-in message via WhatsApp. The
student replies. The teacher intervenes before the student drops off.
The teacher thinks: "*this tool just saved me a student.*"

### Teacher bliss 3 — First cohort trend upward

After a teacher pushes a concept + teaches it well, they see the
concept's cohort mastery climb in their dashboard. Direct causal
feedback on their teaching. "*what I did actually worked.*"

## 8. What this means for different deployment modes

### Self-study deployment (no teachers)

Admin deploys, no teachers assigned to anyone. All students are
taught by AI. Experience is identical to v2.5-v2.8: every student
is a self-studier.

### Coaching institute deployment

Admin promotes several teachers, assigns students. Teachers use `/teaching`
dashboard. Students see their teacher card and announcements. The
coaching institute's human teachers remain central; AI is their tool.

### Hybrid deployment

Some students assigned to teachers, some not. Both modes coexist
gracefully. Self-studiers don't see teacher UI; teacher-assigned
students see it. Same deployment, two experience modes.
