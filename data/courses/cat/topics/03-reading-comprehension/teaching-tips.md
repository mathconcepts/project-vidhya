# Teaching Tips — Reading Comprehension (CAT)

## For Students

### The Core Truth
RC in CAT isn't testing your vocabulary or general knowledge — it's testing whether you can find the right answer **within the passage**. The answer is always in the text; your job is to locate it, not remember it.

### The 3-Read Strategy
1. **Quick scan (1 min)**: Read first and last line of every paragraph. Build a mental map of the passage structure.
2. **Read questions first**: Skim all questions *before* reading deeply — know what you're hunting for.
3. **Active read (3–4 min)**: Read fully, mentally tagging each paragraph with one word (e.g., "claim", "evidence", "counter", "conclusion").

### Common Mistakes & Fixes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Reading every word slowly | Read for structure — paragraphs, not sentences |
| Choosing "true but not in passage" options | Every correct answer must be traceable to specific lines |
| Picking extreme options ("always", "never") | CAT RC rarely has extreme claims — they're usually traps |
| Re-reading full passage for each question | Tag paragraphs on first read; navigate directly |
| Spending 10+ min on one passage | If stuck on a question after 2 passes, mark and move on |

### Question Type Strategies

**Main Idea / Central Theme**: Your one-word paragraph tags should give you this. What does the whole passage argue?

**Inference**: Look for the "one step beyond" answer — not stated directly, but logically follows. Eliminate clearly stated (fact) options and clearly unsupported (assumption) options.

**Tone / Attitude**: Look for loaded words (critical, celebratory, ambivalent, ironic). The author's tone is usually in the opening and closing paragraphs.

**Specific Detail**: This is a "find and verify" question. Go to the relevant paragraph, find the line, match it to the option verbatim.

**Vocabulary in Context**: Substitute each option into the sentence. Pick the one that makes grammatical and logical sense in context — ignore the "dictionary definition" trap.

### Time Management
- 24 VARC questions in 40 minutes = ~1.7 min/question
- 4 RC passages with 3–4 questions each = spend ~6–7 min per passage
- Attempt all RC questions — well-read students can score 20+ here

### Analogies That Help
- Think of RC like a **courtroom**: you need **evidence from the text** to justify every answer choice. Personal opinion = inadmissible.
- Each paragraph is a **chapter in a short story** — after reading it all, you should be able to summarise each chapter in one sentence.

## For Teachers / Tutors

### What CAT RC Actually Tests
CAT RC is not a language test — it's a **logic and attention test**. Students who struggle usually have one of two problems:
1. They read slowly / re-read constantly (fixable with practice)
2. They pick answers that "sound right" instead of verifying in the passage (fixable with answer-tracing drills)

### High-Leverage Teaching Techniques

**Technique 1: The One-Sentence Summary Drill**
After each paragraph, have students write a 1-sentence summary before continuing. This forces active reading and builds the mental map they need for questions.

**Technique 2: Answer Tracing**
For every correct answer in a practice set, require students to **highlight the exact line in the passage** that supports it. If they can't trace it, the answer is wrong (even if it's in the key). This breaks the "sounds true" habit.

**Technique 3: Elimination Speed Drills**
Give 4 options, 30 seconds. Which two can you eliminate fastest? Practice eliminating by:
- Too extreme (never/always/completely)
- Out of scope (true in general, but not what the passage says)
- Reversed logic (opposite of what the author claims)

**Technique 4: Passage Mapping**
Have students draw a simple flowchart of the passage after reading: Main Claim → Supporting Point 1 → Counter-argument → Author's Resolution. This is especially useful for abstract/philosophical passages (CAT's favourite).

### Common Misconceptions to Address
- "I'm a slow reader, so RC will always be hard" — Speed improves with structured practice; most students improve 30–40% in 4 weeks
- "I should read the whole passage twice" — One focused read + targeted re-reads is faster
- "The longest option is usually right" — CAT deliberately uses long options as traps; length ≠ correctness

### Passage Types in CAT (and approach for each)
| Type | Approach |
|------|----------|
| Philosophy/Abstract | Focus on author's stance; 3–4 opinion markers per paragraph |
| Science/Tech | Focus on cause-effect chains; ignore jargon, look for what it *does* |
| Economics/Business | Look for the problem and the proposed solution |
| Literary/Cultural | Tone is key; pay attention to adjectives and metaphors |
| Historical | Timeline often matters; note "before" and "after" markers |

### Assessment Rubric
- **Beginner**: Can identify main idea; struggles with inference
- **Intermediate**: Correct on detail + main idea; 50% on inference/tone
- **Advanced**: 80%+ accuracy across all question types within time limits
- **CAT-ready**: 85%+ on 4 passages in 28 minutes with full verification
