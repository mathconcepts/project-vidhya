# Quantitative Aptitude — CAT Lecture Notes

## What This Section Tests

CAT Quant is not a test of raw calculation speed — it is a test of **mathematical reasoning and pattern recognition**. The IIMs want to know if you can structure ambiguous problems, choose the most elegant method, and work efficiently under time pressure. Expect 24 questions in 40 minutes (CAT 2024 format), of which roughly 8 are TITA (non-MCQ, no negative marking).

The section covers five broad areas: Number Systems, Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry & Mensuration, and Modern Math (Permutation & Combination, Probability, Sets & Functions).

---

## Core Concepts and Sub-Topics

### 1. Number Systems
The bedrock of CAT Quant. Key ideas:
- **Divisibility rules** (2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11)
- **HCF and LCM**: HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers (for two numbers only)
- **Remainders**: Cyclicity of units digit (powers of 2, 3, 7, 9 repeat in cycles of 4); Fermat's Little Theorem; Chinese Remainder Theorem for advanced problems
- **Factors**: Number of factors of N = pᵃqᵇrᶜ... is (a+1)(b+1)(c+1)...
- **Perfect squares and cubes**: Recognising these saves enormous time
- **Base conversions**: Binary to decimal conversions appear occasionally

### 2. Arithmetic
This is the highest-yield area for most students. Sub-topics:
- **Ratio & Proportion**: Partnership, mixture & alligation (the cross-method is fastest)
- **Percentages**: Think in terms of fractions (20% = 1/5, 12.5% = 1/8)
- **Profit & Loss**: SP = CP × (1 ± profit%/100); successive discounts via multiplier method
- **Simple & Compound Interest**: SI = PNR/100; CI uses the formula or binomial expansion for 2-3 years
- **Time & Work**: Assume total work = LCM of individual days; work-rate = 1/days
- **Time, Speed & Distance**: Distance = Speed × Time; Relative speed (same direction = difference, opposite = sum); boats & streams

### 3. Algebra
- **Linear & Quadratic equations**: Vieta's formulas (sum of roots = -b/a, product = c/a)
- **Inequalities**: Key property — multiplying by a negative flips the inequality; AM-GM inequality
- **Functions**: Even/odd functions, composition, domain-range
- **Progressions**: AP sum = n/2(2a + (n-1)d); GP sum = a(rⁿ-1)/(r-1); infinite GP = a/(1-r) for |r|<1
- **Logarithms**: logₐ(xy) = logₐx + logₐy; change of base; log₁₀2 ≈ 0.301

### 4. Geometry & Mensuration
- **Triangles**: Pythagoras, similarity criteria (AA, SAS, SSS), area = ½ × base × height, Hero's formula
- **Circles**: Tangent properties, chord-angle theorems, arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr
- **Quadrilaterals**: Properties of parallelogram, rhombus, trapezium
- **3D Mensuration**: Cube, cuboid, cylinder, cone, sphere surface areas and volumes
- **Coordinate Geometry**: Distance formula, section formula, slope, equation of a circle

### 5. Modern Math
- **P&C**: nPr = n!/(n-r)!; nCr = n!/(r!(n-r)!); circular permutations = (n-1)!
- **Probability**: P(A∪B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A∩B); conditional probability
- **Sets**: Inclusion-exclusion principle for 2 and 3 sets
- **Functions & Logarithms**: Often tested in combination with inequalities

## Key CAT-Specific Strategies

**1. Use Options Aggressively**
CAT options are designed to trap you if you solve mechanically. Always glance at the options first — if they are widely spaced (1, 10, 100, 1000), estimation is faster than exact calculation.

**2. Master Fractions Over Decimals**
Convert 16.67% to 1/6, 11.11% to 1/9. Fraction arithmetic is 2-3x faster than decimal computation on paper.

**3. Identify TITA Questions**
TITA questions have no negative marking. If you can narrow down to a range, always attempt TITA. For MCQ, if you cannot eliminate at least 2 options, skip.

**4. Two-Pass Strategy**
Spend 25 minutes attempting the 15 questions you find comfortable. Use the remaining 15 minutes for TITA attempts and a second pass on tricky problems. Never spend more than 3 minutes on a single question.

**5. Spot the Trap Early**
CAT setters love to embed a trap in the last step — a question about the "number of values" when you calculated "the value", or a question about the area of a shaded region when you calculated the whole shape.

## Common Traps and Mistakes

- **Profit % on CP vs SP**: Always default to Cost Price unless stated otherwise
- **Ratio problems**: Ensure you are working with the same units
- **Logarithm base confusion**: log without base means log₁₀ in CAT context
- **Circular permutation**: Do not forget the (n-1)! formula
- **Compound interest "per annum"**: If half-yearly, double the rate becomes rate/2 and periods double
- **Signs in quadratic**: Vieta's formulas have a sign — sum of roots is -b/a, not +b/a

## Time Management Tips

- **Target**: 16 correct answers out of 24 to reach ~85th percentile in Quant
- **Ideal split**: ~22 questions attempted, ~16 correct
- **Question difficulty identification**: Easy (< 1 min), Medium (1-2.5 min), Hard (> 2.5 min — skip on first pass)
- **Personal timer**: Keep a mental clock; if you have been on a question for 2 minutes with no clear path, mark and move
- **Geometry questions**: Draw neat diagrams; 80% of geometry errors come from misreading the figure

---

## Summary

CAT Quant rewards those who know fewer concepts very deeply and apply them creatively. You do not need to memorise 200 formulas — you need to know 30 well and see which one fits a new problem shape. Practice 20 questions daily, review every mistake to identify the concept gap, and simulate full mock exams every weekend.
