# GATE Engineering Mathematics — Official Syllabus

> Based on GATE 2024 official notification. Valid for CS, EC, EE, ME, CE, CH, IN streams.

---

## Syllabus Overview

Engineering Mathematics is a **common section** across most GATE papers. It tests fundamental mathematical concepts essential for engineering. Historically, this section has been **predictable** — the same core topics appear year after year, making it one of the **highest ROI** sections to prepare.

---

## 📐 Topic 1: Linear Algebra

**Weightage: 12–15% | Expected Marks: 3–4 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Matrices and Determinants**
  - Types of matrices (symmetric, skew-symmetric, orthogonal, Hermitian)
  - Determinant properties and computation
  - Adjoint and inverse of a matrix
  - Rank of a matrix
  
- **Systems of Linear Equations**
  - Consistency conditions (Rouché–Capelli theorem)
  - Homogeneous and non-homogeneous systems
  - Solution using Gaussian elimination
  
- **Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors**
  - Characteristic equation: det(A − λI) = 0
  - Properties of eigenvalues (trace, determinant relations)
  - Cayley-Hamilton theorem
  - Diagonalization
  
- **Vector Spaces**
  - Linear independence, basis, dimension
  - Null space, column space, row space

**GATE Focus:** Eigenvalue problems, rank-nullity, system consistency appear almost every year.

---

## 📈 Topic 2: Calculus

**Weightage: 8–10% | Expected Marks: 2–3 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Limits and Continuity**
  - ε-δ definition
  - One-sided limits, continuity conditions
  - Intermediate Value Theorem

- **Differential Calculus**
  - Derivatives of standard functions
  - Chain rule, product rule, quotient rule
  - Mean Value Theorem, Rolle's Theorem
  - Taylor and Maclaurin series
  - Maxima and minima (single variable)

- **Integral Calculus**
  - Definite and indefinite integrals
  - Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
  - Techniques: substitution, integration by parts, partial fractions

- **Multivariable Calculus**
  - Partial derivatives, total derivative
  - Jacobian
  - Double and triple integrals
  - Change of variables (polar, cylindrical, spherical)

**GATE Focus:** Taylor series, partial derivatives, definite integrals with substitutions.

---

## 🌀 Topic 3: Differential Equations

**Weightage: 8–10% | Expected Marks: 2–3 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **First-Order ODEs**
  - Separable equations
  - Linear first-order ODEs (integrating factor method)
  - Exact equations

- **Second-Order Linear ODEs**
  - Homogeneous equations with constant coefficients
  - Characteristic equation (real, complex, repeated roots)
  - Method of undetermined coefficients
  - Variation of parameters

- **Higher-Order ODEs**
  - Reduction of order
  - Euler-Cauchy equations

- **Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)**
  - Classification: elliptic, parabolic, hyperbolic
  - Heat equation, wave equation, Laplace equation
  - Method of separation of variables

**GATE Focus:** Second-order ODE solutions, PDE classification, initial value problems.

---

## 🔢 Topic 4: Complex Variables

**Weightage: 6–8% | Expected Marks: 1–2 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Complex Number Algebra**
  - Modulus, argument, polar form
  - De Moivre's theorem

- **Analytic Functions**
  - Cauchy-Riemann equations
  - Harmonic functions

- **Complex Integration**
  - Cauchy's integral theorem
  - Cauchy's integral formula
  - Residue theorem

- **Power Series**
  - Taylor series in complex domain
  - Laurent series
  - Singularities (removable, pole, essential)

**GATE Focus:** C-R equations, contour integration, residues at poles.

---

## 🎲 Topic 5: Probability & Statistics

**Weightage: 10–12% | Expected Marks: 2–3 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Probability Theory**
  - Sample space, events, axioms
  - Conditional probability
  - Bayes' theorem
  - Independence

- **Random Variables**
  - Discrete RVs (Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Geometric)
  - Continuous RVs (Uniform, Exponential, Normal/Gaussian)
  - CDF, PDF, PMF

- **Expectation and Variance**
  - Mean, variance, standard deviation
  - Moments and moment generating functions

- **Statistics**
  - Sampling distributions
  - Estimation (point and interval)
  - Hypothesis testing basics

**GATE Focus:** Bayes' theorem, Poisson/Normal distributions, conditional probability.

---

## 🔢 Topic 6: Numerical Methods

**Weightage: 6–8% | Expected Marks: 1–2 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Root Finding**
  - Bisection method
  - Newton-Raphson method
  - Secant method

- **Interpolation**
  - Lagrange interpolation
  - Newton's divided difference
  - Newton's forward/backward difference

- **Numerical Integration**
  - Trapezoidal rule
  - Simpson's 1/3 and 3/8 rules
  - Gaussian quadrature

- **Numerical ODE Solutions**
  - Euler's method
  - Runge-Kutta methods (RK4)

- **Linear Systems**
  - Gauss-Seidel iteration
  - LU decomposition

**GATE Focus:** Newton-Raphson convergence, Simpson's rule, Euler/RK4 methods.

---

## 🔄 Topic 7: Transform Theory

**Weightage: 6–8% | Expected Marks: 1–2 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Laplace Transform**
  - Definition and standard transforms
  - Properties (linearity, shifting, scaling)
  - Inverse Laplace transform
  - Solving ODEs with Laplace transforms
  - Convolution theorem

- **Fourier Series**
  - Trigonometric Fourier series
  - Dirichlet conditions
  - Parseval's theorem
  - Even/odd function series

- **Fourier Transform**
  - Fourier transform pair definitions
  - Properties (linearity, time-shifting, frequency-shifting)
  - Convolution in frequency domain

- **Z-Transform** *(CS/EC specific)*
  - Definition, ROC
  - Inverse Z-transform

**GATE Focus:** Laplace transforms of standard functions, Fourier series coefficients.

---

## 🔣 Topic 8: Discrete Mathematics

**Weightage: 8–10% | Expected Marks: 2–3 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Logic and Proofs**
  - Propositional logic, truth tables
  - Predicate logic, quantifiers
  - Proof techniques (direct, contradiction, induction)

- **Set Theory**
  - Set operations, Venn diagrams
  - Power set, Cartesian product
  - Countability

- **Relations**
  - Properties (reflexive, symmetric, transitive, antisymmetric)
  - Equivalence relations, partial orders
  - Hasse diagrams

- **Functions**
  - Injective, surjective, bijective
  - Composition, inverse functions

- **Combinatorics**
  - Permutations and combinations
  - Pigeonhole principle
  - Inclusion-exclusion principle
  - Generating functions

- **Boolean Algebra**
  - Boolean identities
  - Sum of products, product of sums
  - Karnaugh maps (for CE/CS)

**GATE Focus:** Logic equivalences, counting problems, relation properties.

---

## 🕸️ Topic 9: Graph Theory

**Weightage: 5–7% | Expected Marks: 1–2 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Graph Basics**
  - Undirected and directed graphs
  - Degree sequence, handshaking lemma
  - Paths, cycles, connectivity

- **Special Graphs**
  - Bipartite graphs
  - Complete graphs K_n
  - Planar graphs, Euler's formula
  - Trees and spanning trees

- **Graph Algorithms**
  - BFS, DFS traversal
  - Minimum spanning tree (Kruskal, Prim)
  - Shortest path (Dijkstra, Bellman-Ford)

- **Graph Properties**
  - Euler and Hamiltonian paths/circuits
  - Graph coloring, chromatic number
  - Matching in bipartite graphs

**GATE Focus:** Spanning trees, planar graph properties, Euler circuits.

---

## ∇ Topic 10: Vector Calculus

**Weightage: 5–7% | Expected Marks: 1–2 out of 100**

### Sub-topics:
- **Vectors and Vector Fields**
  - Vector algebra, dot and cross products
  - Scalar and vector fields

- **Differential Operators**
  - Gradient (∇f)
  - Divergence (∇·F)
  - Curl (∇×F)
  - Laplacian (∇²f)

- **Line Integrals**
  - Work done by a force field
  - Conservative fields, potential functions

- **Surface and Volume Integrals**
  - Surface integrals of scalar and vector fields
  - Volume integrals

- **Integral Theorems**
  - Green's theorem (2D)
  - Stokes' theorem (3D)
  - Gauss's Divergence theorem

**GATE Focus:** Gradient/curl/divergence computations, Green's/Stokes'/Divergence theorems.
