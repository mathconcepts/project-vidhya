The derivative measures how fast a function is changing at a point. If $f(x)$ tells you your position at time $x$, then $f'(x)$ tells you your velocity — how much your position changes per tiny change in time.

Picture the graph of $f$. At any point, zoom in until the curve looks like a straight line. The slope of that line is the derivative. Steep line = big derivative. Flat line = derivative near zero. Going downhill = negative derivative.

Given any differentiable $f$, its derivative $f'$ is another function, defined at every point where the original function is "smooth enough" to have a well-defined rate of change.
