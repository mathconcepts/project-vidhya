The derivative is the slope of the curve at every point. As $t$ advances, watch the curve $y = \sin(x + t)$ slide horizontally — at any vertical line, the slope you see is exactly $f'(x) = \cos(x + t)$.

This is what a derivative *looks like*: not a number, not a formula, but the local steepness of a curve at every position, animated in time so you can feel how it changes as the function evolves.
